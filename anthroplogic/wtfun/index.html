LIVE 3/6 : WTF(UN) IS «COLIMIT» OR «HIGHER INDUCTIVE FAMILY» ? | JARGON OF MATHEMATICIANS
FRIDAY 21 FEBRUARY 2020 @ 22:22 GMT+1

In short : the jargon "colimit" / "coproduct" in geometry corresponds to the jargon "higher inductive family" in logic . But in contrast to "colimit" , "inductive" allows for self-reference/recursion (via presentation as initial algebra of endofunctor) , and "family" allows for parametrization (via working in the slice over some object) , and "higher" allows to describe more than the "coproduct" "colimits" by moreover touching the arrows/identifications in the base diagram of the "colimit" ( via identification-as-path , but alternatives should be possible ... ) .

LIVE 6/6 : WTF(UN) IS «PATH SPACE» OR «TRANSPORT ALONG EQUALITY» ? | JARGON OF MATHEMATICIANS
FRIDAY 6 MARCH 2020 @ 22:22 GMT+1

In short : the jargon "path space object" in geometry corresponds to the jargon "identity type" / "propositional equality" / "transport along equality" in logic .

In some instances , the "diagonal object" instead of the "path space object" can be used to give sense to the "identity type" , but in the general geometry , the "path space object" is the more precise (internal) sense which also describes the (external) higher equalities beyong the lower diagonal (definitional) equalities ...

The "transport along equality" is the logic that if two things are equal then anything defined-at ( or said-about ) one of them can be transported ( or translated ) along this equality into something defined-at ( or said-about ) the other one . This "transport along equality" logic property corresponds to the geometry property that the morphisms-for-dependent-types are "fibrations" ... SO WE HAVE MOTIVATED FULL CIRCLE !
